
# TCS Prime Interview Experience
## Reporting & Arrival
My reporting time was **12:00 PM**. However, my name was called earlier at around **10:30 AM**, when I had not yet arrived. A friend informed the coordinators that I had not reached the venue yet.

I reached the campus at around **12:10 PM**, completed security verification, and entered the lobby. Due to the large crowd and multiple panels, I initially got confused and spent around **20 minutes** locating the correct interview floor. Eventually, I found out that **Panel 5** interviews were being conducted on the **3rd floor** and reached there by **12:35 PM**.

---

## Waiting Period
After locating the panel coordinator, I waited for nearly **one hour**. Later, it was announced that the panel had gone for lunch. I also took a lunch break and returned by **2:00 PM**.

Interviews resumed after lunch, with each interview lasting approximately **30–35 minutes**.

---

## Interview Duration
- **Start Time:** 2:56 PM  
- **End Time:** 3:25 PM  
- **Total Duration:** ~27 minutes

---

## Interview Panel
The interview panel consisted of:
- **TR (Technical Round)**
- **MR (Managerial Round)**
- **HR (Human Resources)**

---

## Interview Questions & Discussion

### 1. Git / GitHub
I was asked whether I use GitHub and how to switch to a previous version using Git CLI. I mentioned `git checkout`, but the interviewer did not agree with it. After some hesitation, I paused and honestly said that I was not fully sure.

---

### 2. Coding Question – Repeated Characters
I was asked to print repeated characters in my name in the order of their occurrence. I used a **HashMap-based approach**, wrote the logic, and explained the solution clearly.

---

### 3. Pattern Question
I was given a pattern problem for `n = 5` and asked to solve it within **one minute**. I wrote most of the Java logic but could not complete it fully due to time constraints.

```bash
n = 5
* 1 2 3 4 
* * 1 2 3 
* * * 1 2 
* * * * 1 
* * * * *
```
---

### 4. Other Offers
I was asked about other offers. I mentioned my **DBS apprenticeship** starting the following week. I was also asked whether I had any full-time offers and which companies I had applied to. I answered honestly, explaining hiring freezes and outcomes of different applications.

---

### 5. Apprenticeship Details
I was asked:
- Whether the apprenticeship was **paid or unpaid**
- Whether it was **company-funded or self-funded**
- The **stipend amount**

I answered clearly that it was **company-funded and paid**.

---

### 6. Higher Studies
I was asked about plans for pursuing a **master’s degree**. I clearly stated that I do **not** plan to pursue higher studies at this time.

---

### 7. Hypothetical Scenario – Plan B
I was asked what my **Plan B** would be if I were jobless for one year. Initially, I said I would continue searching for good opportunities. When prompted further, I mentioned that I am strong in **mathematics** and could teach **JEE (11th & 12th)** students. I clarified that I currently teach my younger brother.

---

### 8. Technical Concepts
The TR asked:
- A clustering problem involving **red and green balls** (similar to **k-means clustering**)
- **JSON basics**
- Where JSON data is attached in **Postman**
- What **RAG** is and how it works
- How to find the **IP address** of a computer

I answered these questions confidently and the interviewer appeared satisfied.

---

### 9. Questions to the Panel
When asked if I had any questions, I asked about **growth and learning opportunities at TCS**.

---

### 10. Location Preference
The HR asked about preferred locations. I mentioned:
- Hyderabad
- Bangalore
- Mumbai

---

## Conclusion
The interview concluded on a positive note. Apart from the Git command confusion and the incomplete pattern code due to time pressure, the rest of the interview went well. The panel appeared satisfied with my technical understanding, honesty, and overall approach.

---

## Overall Impression
Despite initial delays, confusion, and a long waiting period, I remained calm throughout the process. The interview felt engaging and professional, and I believe I presented myself honestly and confidently.
